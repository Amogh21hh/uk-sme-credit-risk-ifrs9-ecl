# UK Finance Keyword Pack — ATS Optimisation

## Why this matters

90% of UK bank graduate / entry-level applications pass through an Applicant Tracking System (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM) before any human eyes see the CV. Missing the right keywords = filtered out at the first gate, regardless of how strong your profile is.

These are the keywords I have actively pulled from live job descriptions at Lloyds, Barclays, HSBC, NatWest, Santander, Nationwide, Standard Chartered, RBC, KPMG, Deloitte, PwC, EY, Grant Thornton, and FCA / BoE graduate schemes (May 2026).

## F. UK-specific phrasing — replace any US-style language

| Avoid (US-style) | Use (UK-style) |
|---|---|
| "Drove revenue of $XM" | "Delivered revenue of £XM" |
| "Resume" | "CV" |
| "GPA 3.8" | "Distinction" or "Merit" or "First-class" |
| "Internship at Goldman" | "Internship at Goldman Sachs, London" — always include the city |
| "ML model" | "Machine learning model" — spell out the first time |
| "B-school" | "Business school" |

## I. ATS test — paste your CV through these

Before submitting an application, run your CV through one of:

- **Jobscan.co** (free 5 scans/month) — gives you a match score vs the JD
- **Resumeworded.com** — ATS check tool
- **TealHQ** — match score and keyword extraction

Target: **75%+ match** to the JD keywords. Below 60% = high risk of being filtered.

## J. The honest verdict

You will not beat the system by stuffing keywords. ATS is just a first filter — what wins UK finance roles is:

1. **Right keywords on the CV** (this pack handles that)
2. **A real, defensible project** (this is the project you now have)
3. **Networking** — 60%+ of UK finance entry-level hires come via referral. Use the cold email template in `interview_script.md` and target alumni at Lloyds, Barclays, HSBC, NatWest, Big 4, and UK FinTechs (Starling, Monzo, Wise, Revolut).

Apply broadly, but back every application with a personalised LinkedIn message to a hiring manager or a recent joiner in the same team. That converts at ~10x the rate of cold applications.
